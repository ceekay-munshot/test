### Key Positives
- Dual Big 4 joint auditors (SRBC/EY + Deloitte) with clean unqualified reports
- 55% independent directors on board; non-executive chairman (Niraj Bajaj)
- Full remuneration and RPT disclosures per SEBI LODR; MD pay Rs 54.6 crore fully disclosed
- Negligible promoter pledge (0.01%); stable promoter stake ~55%
- High-quality earnings: CFO/EBITDA ~0.85x; negative working capital structure
- Consistent high dividend (Rs 210/share FY25, 54.49% payout ratio)
- Zero debt pre-KTM acquisition; robust standalone FCF generation
- Rajiv Bajaj — highly respected MD & CEO with 35+ year institutional knowledge

### Key Concerns
- KTM AG acquisition (Nov 2025, EUR 800M) on an entity that was insolvent — significant EU-market and FX risk
- Debt-to-equity elevated to 0.58 post-KTM (vs historical near-zero debt)
- Promoter-CEO structure (not independent professional management) — concentration risk
- Promoter group operates multiple other material businesses (Bajaj Finserv, Finance, Mukand)
- Top management tenure moderate (CFO 3 yrs, CTO <1 yr); some second-level churn
- Employee attrition and remuneration benchmarking not explicitly disclosed in AR
